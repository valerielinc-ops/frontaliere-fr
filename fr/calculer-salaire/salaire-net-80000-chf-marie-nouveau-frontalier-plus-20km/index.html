Questions fréquentes

Quel est le salaire net avec CHF 80'000 brut en tant que frontalier?

Un nouveau frontalier avec CHF 80'000 brut perçoit environ CHF 48'783 net par an (CHF 4'065/mois).

Quel barème fiscal s'applique pour CHF 80'000 marié?

Le barème B s'applique avec un taux effectif de 5.1%.

Comment le salaire net est calculé

With a gross annual income of CHF 80'000, the Swiss employer withholds mandatory social contributions of approximately CHF 10'320: AVS (5.3%), unemployment insurance (1.1%), accident insurance (0.7%), daily sickness allowance (0.8%), and occupational pension LPP. The Ticino withholding tax is calculated using table tableB, resulting in an effective rate of 5.1%.

Régime fiscal appliqué

As a new cross-border worker (2024+ agreement), income is subject to concurrent taxation: 100% of the withholding tax stays in Switzerland. Italian IRPEF applies with a €10,000 deduction and proportional tax credit for Swiss taxes paid.

Impact de la situation familiale

As a married person, tax table B applies with reduced rates. Swiss family allowances amount to CHF 0/year.

Zone de distance frontalière

Living over 20 km from the border, 100% of the withholding tax stays in Switzerland. Italian IRPEF applies in full with proportional credit.

Budget mensuel indicatif

Monthly, a Swiss resident nets approximately CHF 4'767, while an Italian cross-border worker receives about EUR 4.456/month (at CHF/EUR 1.096).
