Questions fréquentes

Quel est le salaire net avec CHF 50'000 brut en tant que frontalier?

Un nouveau frontalier avec CHF 50'000 brut perçoit environ CHF 37'288 net par an (CHF 3'107/mois).

Quel barème fiscal s'applique pour CHF 50'000 célibataire?

Le barème H s'applique avec un taux effectif de 0.5%.

Comment le salaire net est calculé

With a gross annual income of CHF 50'000, the Swiss employer withholds mandatory social contributions of approximately CHF 6'450: AVS (5.3%), unemployment insurance (1.1%), accident insurance (0.7%), daily sickness allowance (0.8%), and occupational pension LPP. The Ticino withholding tax is calculated using table tableH, resulting in an effective rate of 0.5%.

Régime fiscal appliqué

As a new cross-border worker (2024+ agreement), income is subject to concurrent taxation: 80% of the withholding tax stays in Switzerland and 20% is returned to Italy. Italian IRPEF applies with a €10,000 deduction and proportional tax credit for Swiss taxes paid.

Impact de la situation familiale

As a single person with 1 child, tax table H applies. Swiss family allowances: CHF 3'000/year.

Zone de distance frontalière

Living within 20 km of the Swiss border, 80% of the withholding tax stays in Switzerland and 20% goes to your Italian municipality.

Budget mensuel indicatif

Monthly, a Swiss resident nets approximately CHF 3'158, while an Italian cross-border worker receives about EUR 3.406/month (at CHF/EUR 1.096).
